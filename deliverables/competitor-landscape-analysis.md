## Where the Gap Is

None of the competitors above occupy Brett's exact niche. Here is what each lacks:

1. **Thompson / Smith** are text-first and weekly/semi-weekly. They don't do daily audio, and their depth makes them slow. Brett is faster.

2. **Zeihan** is daily and solo but covers only geopolitics and is US-centric in framing. He doesn't touch science or human interest. Brett is broader.

3. **TLDR News** is daily and short but is a scripted team operation with no strong single personality. Brett is more personal.

4. **Morning Brew Daily** is daily audio but requires two hosts and a full production team. Brett is leaner.

5. **Pesca** is daily and solo but runs 30 minutes and doesn't use AI-assisted workflows. Brett is shorter and more efficient.

6. **Bremmer** has the credentials but is backed by a corporate research firm (Eurasia Group). Brett is independent.

7. **Inception Point** uses AI at scale but has zero editorial personality. Brett is the opposite -- AI as tool, human as brand.

**The unclaimed position:** A daily, short-form (5-7 min), multi-topic, solo-personality show where AI handles the production pipeline and the host brings editorial judgment, wit, and a distinctive international perspective. Nobody is doing this combination.

## Positioning Recommendation

### Tagline Concept
*"The smartest 7 minutes of your morning -- curated by AI, delivered by a human who gives a damn."*

### Strategic Positioning

**1. Speed + Personality is the moat.** The AI pipeline lets Brett publish daily across three shows at a cost and speed no solo competitor can match. But the voice, opinion, and editorial taste are irreplaceable. Every episode should feel like a smart friend caught you up over coffee -- not a news bulletin.

**2. Three feeds, one brand.** Launch Politics, Science, and Quirky as separate podcast feeds under one umbrella brand. This lets listeners self-select (most won't want all three) while tripling Brett's surface area for discovery. Cross-promote between shows: "If you liked today's Science episode on deep-ocean microbes, tomorrow on Politics I'm covering the UN seabed mining treaty."

**3. Be the anti-AI-slop.** Inception Point and its imitators are flooding podcast platforms with soulless AI content. Brett should lean into transparency: "I use AI to find stories, draft research, and edit audio -- but every opinion, every joke, every editorial call is mine." This builds trust with the exact audience (35-60 professionals) who are skeptical of AI-generated content.

**4. Start free, monetise with depth.** For the first 6 months, everything is free. Build the daily habit. Then introduce a paid tier: a weekly 15-20 min "deep dive" episode, an email digest with links and context, or early-access to episodes. The Noah Smith / Ben Thompson model proves intellectually curious audiences will pay $8-12/month for analysis they trust.

**5. Own the "international generalist" angle.** Zeihan is American. Thompson is American-in-Taiwan. Bremmer is American. Brett is Australian in Barcelona -- genuinely international, no default US lens. This is rare in English-language commentary and directly appeals to the target audience (international professionals who are tired of US-centric framing).

**6. Short-form social as discovery engine.** Extract 60-second clips from each episode for Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and LinkedIn. Bremmer and Zeihan both drive massive discovery through short clips. At Brett's budget, AI tools (Opus Clip, Descript) can automate this from each episode.

### Priority Actions (First 30 Days)

- Launch all three feeds on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube (audio + static image)
- Establish the 5-7 min format with a consistent structure: hook (15 sec) + 2-3 stories (90 sec each) + sign-off with teaser for next episode
- Create 60-second clips from every episode for social distribution
- Build an email list from day one (even a simple "subscribe for episode links" landing page)
- Explicitly brand the AI-assisted workflow as a feature, not a secret
