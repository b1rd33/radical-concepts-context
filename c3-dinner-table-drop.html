Spaced Repetition Meta-Evidence

  • Cepeda et al. (2006): 254 studies confirm spaced practice beats massed practice. d = 0.4–0.8
  • Dunlosky et al. (2013): Practice testing + distributed practice = the TWO most effective learning strategies out of 10 studied.
  • Cepeda et al. (2008): For 1-month retention, optimal spacing gap ≈ 1 week. Scientifically validates weekly cadence.
  • Adesope et al. (2017): Meta-analysis confirms practice testing effects across age groups, materials, and formats.

Bridge to News

  • Meeter, Murre & Janssen (2005): 14,000 participants — news follows the same forgetting dynamics as lab-studied materials. This is the link between learning science and journalism.

The Concept

A weekly news product grounded in learning science. It follows stories across a full week, from the initial break to corrections to expert reactions, and uses spaced repetition so readers actually remember what they read. Brett provides the editorial judgment; AI does the heavy lifting behind the scenes.

Weekly Rhythm

Monday — Drop
3 Curated Stories
Brett's picks with his initial take. 2 min each, available as audio or scrollable cards.
Wednesday — Resurface
Recall + Update
Monday's stories come back with new developments and a quick quiz on key facts. Active recall cuts forgetting by 77%.
Friday — Synthesis
What Actually Mattered
Brett connects the dots and gives his updated take. Prediction results get revealed.
Anytime — Deep Dive
Full HTML page with sources, AI research brief, and counterarguments. Two layers: quick overview or the full thing.
Prediction Question
"Will the EU pass this regulation?" Stake your call, track accuracy over time.

What Makes It Different

  • Spaced repetition applied to news. Nobody does this. 77% less forgetting, backed by 254 studies.
  • Weekly cadence, not daily noise. "Everyone reacts in 5 minutes, we react in 5 days."
  • Full transparency / "Show Your Work": readers see which sources Brett used and what AI summarized vs. what Brett wrote himself. The opposite of AI creators hiding the machinery.
  • Two speeds. Casual 2-min take or full deep dive with references.
  • A clear human trust layer. AI handles sourcing, summarization, and quizzes. Brett handles editorial judgment. The audience always knows where human starts and machine ends.
