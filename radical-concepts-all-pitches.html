Concept 1 — Brett on Autopilot

1. Production Pipeline & Cost Architecture

The recommended stack is n8n (self-hosted) + GPT-4o-mini + GPT-4o + ElevenLabs + Buzzsprout, costing ~$55–85/month at mid-range or ~$150–220/month at premium. The pipeline has six layers: RSS ingestion (monitoring 14+ sources), AI summarization, script generation, TTS voice synthesis, audio post-processing, and automated distribution. TTS dominates the budget — LLM costs are under $5/month even with premium models.

Realistic human oversight is 30–60 minutes/day for script review and audio QA. Zero-oversight pipelines show 68–84% failure rates (see Washington Post below).

  • Benchmark Traditional production: 4–8 hours. AI-assisted hybrid: 1–3 hours. Fully automated: 5–15 minutes.
  • Benchmark Total cost: $50–200/month vs traditional $500–2,000+ per episode.
  • Source n8n documentation · ElevenLabs pricing · Buzzsprout pricing

2. Voice Technology & Listener Perception

Voice cloning can pass detection tests, but audience acceptance is a separate and unresolved problem. A 2025 PLoS One study found voice clones fooled 58% of listeners — but Sounds Profitable’s 2025 report found 47% of podcast consumers would be less likely to keep listening to a show using AI voices.

  • Data 56% of listeners say podcast hosts are the influencer type that “matters most” — 3× the share for social media influencers (Cumulus/Signal Hill 2025)
  • Data AI voices reduce cognitive engagement at the neural level vs. human voices (PMC EEG study, 2023)
  • Source Lavan et al. PLoS One 2025 · Sounds Profitable 2025 · PMC EEG study

3. Failure Cases & Quality Benchmarks

Washington Post AI Podcast (Dec 2025): 68–84% of scripts failed editorial standards. Fabricated quotes, misattributed statements. The most important cautionary case for C1.

What worked: Solo creators who maintained human oversight succeeded. The Newsworthy reached 800K downloads/month by year 5 with a staff of one.

  • Source Semafor investigation · American Press Institute

4. Content Licensing & Legal Risk

70+ active AI copyright lawsuits as of late 2025. Key cases: Advance Local v. Cohere (AI summaries may infringe even if non-verbatim), Bartz v. Anthropic ($1.5B settlement), NYT v. OpenAI (ongoing).

Safe models: Morning Brew ($75M exit), 1440 (4M+ subs), Axios ($525M exit) all use human editorial rewriting — extract facts, rewrite in original voice, link to sources.

  • Source Advance Local v. Cohere (S.D.N.Y., Nov 2025) · Bartz v. Anthropic ($1.5B settlement) · NYT v. OpenAI (Dec 2023) · Tennessee ELVIS Act (2024) · EU AI Act

5. Competitive Landscape & Market Data

Market: Global podcast market $31.49B (2025). AI in podcasting $4.06B with 32.2% CAGR. The Daily costs ~$23–29K/episode with 50+ staff; C1 produces comparable format at <$10/episode.

Gap: Most daily news podcasts require large teams OR sacrifice quality. The combination of AI-assisted production + human editorial voice + daily cadence is underserved.

  • Source Grand View Research · NYRB May 2025 · Spotify guidelines · Apple guidelines

6. Monetization & Break-Even Economics

At $25 CPM mid-roll with 2 ads/episode, break-even is ~150–200 downloads/episode. Daily cadence gives 5.5× more ad inventory than weekly shows. Reality: median podcast gets <50 downloads/episode; $1,000/month requires top-5% performance.

  • Source IAB CPM benchmarks · Buzzsprout industry data · The Newsworthy public data

Concept 2 — The Curiosity Game

1. Retention Science & the Testing Effect

Quizzing people on material makes them remember 25–50% more than passive reading. Adesope et al. 2017 (272 studies): testing effect d = 0.56. Roediger & Karpicke 2006: passive readers forgot 56% after one week while tested readers forgot only 13%.

  • Source Adesope et al. 2017 · Karpicke & Blunt 2011 (Science) · Roediger & Karpicke 2006

2. Gamification Mechanics — Duolingo as the Model

Duolingo’s DAU grew 4.5× in three years (13M → 50M+). Revenue exceeded $1B+ annually by end 2025.

  • Data Streaks — 3× more likely to return daily, 60% commitment increase
  • Data Leaderboards — 40% engagement lift from social competition
  • Data DAU/MAU ratio — 37%, exceptional for any consumer app
  • Source Duolingo Q4 2025 earnings · Yu-kai Chou Octalysis · BJ Fogg B=MAP · Nir Eyal Hook Model

3. Doom Scrolling & the Passive Consumption Problem

64% of Americans identify as doomscrollers (Payless Power 2025). Over 60% of app sessions diverge from the user’s original intention (CMU study, 34K screenshots). The gap isn’t access to information — it’s that no product makes learning as easy and habit-forming as scrolling.

  • Source Satici et al. 2023 · Payless Power 2025 · Harris Poll 2024

4. Social Currency & Viral Sharing

Berger & Milkman 2012: awe-inspiring science content is the #1 most-shared category online. Loewenstein 1994: curiosity = gap between what you know and want to know. A quiz format exploits this gap perfectly.

  • Benchmark Wordle — 5.3B plays in 2024, 99% next-day return
  • Benchmark Spotify Wrapped — 500M shares annually
  • Benchmark NYT Games — 11.1B plays, daily habit + share culture
  • Source Berger & Milkman 2012 · Loewenstein 1994 · Wohn & Ahmadi 2019

5. Competitive Landscape & the White Space

No product combines daily + science + quiz + shareable. 15+ products scanned across daily games, science education, gamified learning, and shareable content.

  • Competitor Kurzgesagt (24.8M subs) — Science content, but video not interactive
  • Competitor Wordle (NYT) — Daily shareable, but word puzzles not knowledge
  • Competitor Duolingo (50M+ DAU) — Gamified daily learning, but languages not curiosity
  • Competitor Brilliant.org ($100M+ raised) — Closest in content, but not daily, no share mechanic
  • Competitor Columbia “Science Scramble” — Closest direct competitor, archived Feb 2024
  • Case Kahoot (9B+ participants) — Simple format, pivoted to B2B. 70% of Fortune 500. Validates quiz-to-B2B path.

6. B2B Corporate Learning Market

Corporate e-learning: $104–125B market. Microlearning segment: $2.6B → $10.4B by 2032. No one has “daily gamified curiosity content” as a B2B employee engagement tool.

  • Data Employees get only 24 minutes/week for learning — 1% of work time (Bersin/Deloitte)
  • Data Microlearning: 25–60% higher retention, 130% productivity lift vs. traditional
  • Data C2 recommended pricing: $3–7/user/month ($36–84/year)
  • Source Grand View Research · Training Industry Report 2025 · Bersin by Deloitte

7. Science YouTube Audience & Creator-Product Fit

Science YouTube viewers are 65–93% male, concentrated in 25–36 age band, disproportionately college-educated (Landrum 2021). 73% of adults self-identify as “lifelong learners” (Pew).

  • Case CrunchLabs (Mark Rober) — YouTube to ~$10M/month in subscriptions
  • Case Brilliant.org — Sponsored 208+ science channels, validates paying segment
  • Data At 0.05–0.10% conversion, launch yields 5,000–20,000 installs. LTV:CAC 12:1+
  • Source Landrum 2021 · Pew Research · CrunchLabs/Brilliant.org public data

Concept 3 — The Dinner Table Drop

1. Spaced Repetition & Memory Science

Roediger & Karpicke 2006: passive readers forgot 56% while tested readers forgot only 13% — a 77% reduction in forgetting. Meeter et al. 2005 (14,000 participants, 500K data points) proved news follows the same forgetting dynamics. Higher consumption does NOT slow forgetting.

  • Data Cepeda et al. (2008): optimal gap for 1-month retention is ~1 week — supports DTD’s weekly cadence
  • Data Adesope et al. (2017): testing effect d = 0.56, 25–50% retention improvement
  • Source Roediger & Karpicke 2006 · Meeter et al. 2005 · Adesope et al. 2017 · Murre & Dros 2015

2. Trust in Media & News Consumption

Trust has collapsed, avoidance is rising, analysis infrastructure is disappearing — but demand for depth is growing.

  • Data 28% of Americans trust mass media — Gallup 2025, all-time low since 1972
  • Data 40% of global audiences actively avoid news — Reuters 2025, up from 29% in 2017
  • Data 3,500 US newspapers closed since 2005; 213 counties are now news deserts
  • Source Gallup 2025 · Reuters DNR 2025 · Edelman Trust Barometer 2025/2026

3. News Retention & the “Illusion of Being Informed”

After watching a full newscast, the average viewer recalls just 6% of headlines unaided (1 in 16). 75% are overconfident about their news judgment (Pennycook & Rand 2021). 75% of news articles shared on social media are shared without clicking the link.

  • Source Bäuml 2021 · Pennycook & Rand 2021 · Penn State 2020

4. News Avoidance Psychology

40% of global audiences avoid news (Reuters 2025). Reasons: mood impact 39%, worn out 31%, too much conflict 30%. Research shows selective avoiders are actively open to formats that are explanatory, curated, and solutions-oriented.

  • Data The Trust Project: ~300 news orgs, trust jumped 8% after implementing transparency indicators
  • Case Tortoise Media — “ThinkIn” events where public participates in editorial decisions
  • Source Reuters DNR 2023–2025 · Toff & Palmer 2019 · The Trust Project

5. Competitive White Space — 50+ Product Scan

Zero products combine all four axes: spaced repetition, quizzes on news, human editorial curation, and source transparency.

  • Competitor Morning Brew (4M+ subs, acquired ~$75M) — No depth, no retention
  • Competitor Tortoise Media (110K+ paying members) — Closest in philosophy, no learning science
  • Competitor Ground News (1M+ users) — Transparency via bias ratings, zero retention mechanics
  • Competitor Stratechery ($15/mo, 40K+ paid) — Deep analysis, no retention mechanics
  • Competitor GeoBarta (launched 2026) — Only partial overlap; geography not news, no editorial curation

6. Newsletter Economics & Monetization

Personality-driven newsletters convert 6–12% free-to-paid vs. 2–5% for brand-driven. Annual subscriber survival: 70% for annual plans vs. 34% for monthly (FT Strategies).

  • Case The Free Press — 170K paid / 1.5M total = 11.3% conversion, ~$20M/year
  • Case Morning Brew — Acquired ~$75M · Axios — Acquired $525M
  • Data C3 model: $10/month, 3–5% conversion from 10K list = $36K–$60K/year in Year 1
  • Source Substack/ConvertKit benchmarks · Reuters “Paying for News” 2023 · FT Strategies

7. Audience & Demographics

News payers skew older, educated, higher income: 60% male, 79% medium-to-high income. Brett’s science YouTube audience (65–93% male, 25–36 age band) maps directly onto the demographic that already pays for online news.

  • Source Reuters DNR 2023–2025 · Pew 2025–2026 · Landrum 2021
