The Curiosity Game

A daily interactive game that turns science and quirky stories into social currency, so you're the most interesting person at lunch.

The Problem

Intellectually curious people consume hours of content daily and walk away with nothing to show for it. They doom-scroll, skim headlines, half-read articles, and at lunch or dinner have nothing fascinating to say.

Retained for conversation

The gap isn't information access. It's the gap between consuming content and being able to use it in real conversation. Content is passive, forgettable, optimized for clicks rather than retention. There's no "smart alternative" to doom scrolling that's equally easy and addictive but actually leaves you with something.

The Dinner Table Gap

From our pain-point analysis: people want to be the person who drops something fascinating at lunch, but they don't have curated, memorable material to pull from. The problem isn't curiosity. Nothing they consume is designed to be remembered or shared.

Research & Insights

Gamification Science: Duolingo as Proof

  • DAU growth: 4.5x from 2022–2025 (~13M to 50M+ daily active users) (Duolingo earnings, 2025)
  • DAU/MAU ratio: 37%, best-in-class for consumer apps (Duolingo Q4 2024)
  • Streak users 3x more likely to return daily; 60% commitment increase (Duolingo product blog)
  • Leaderboard engagement lift: 40%; churn declined from 47% to 37% (Duolingo 2024 annual)
  • Revenue surpassed $1B+ annually by end 2025 (Duolingo FY2025)

The Testing Effect: Scientific Backbone

  • Meta-analysis effect size: d = 0.56 (medium-to-large), meaning quizzing improves retention 25–50% over passive reading (Adesope et al., 2017, 272 studies)
  • Works specifically for factual information, which is exactly what science/news content is
  • Delayed testing more effective than immediate testing
  • Microlearning completion rates: ~80% vs ~20% for traditional formats (ATD Research)

Five Behavioral Frameworks Applied

  • Octalysis (Yu-kai Chou): Epic Meaning + Accomplishment + Social Influence + Unpredictability
  • Fogg B=MAP: Motivation (curiosity) × Ability (3 min) × Prompt (push notification)
  • Self-Determination Theory: Autonomy (choose topics) + Competence (correct answers) + Relatedness (share/compete)
  • Flow Theory: Progressive difficulty in 3-min session, immediate feedback
  • Hook Model (Nir Eyal): Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment (streak)

Social Currency Research

  • Berger & Milkman (2012): Awe-inspiring content is the most shared. Content that makes the sharer look knowledgeable drives organic spread. (2,346 citations)
  • STEPPS Framework (Jonah Berger, "Contagious"): Social Currency + Triggers + Emotion + Public + Practical Value + Stories
  • Kurzgesagt: 24.8M subscribers proving demand for accessible science content
  • IFLScience: entire brand built on shareable science curiosity

The Concept

A daily interactive experience that delivers Brett's curated science stories and quirky finds as a 3-minute game. Not a podcast, not a newsletter. You guess, play, react, and share. The goal is to replace doom scrolling with something that actually makes you more interesting.

B2C
Curious individuals bored of scrolling
B2B
Companies & universities licensing for teams
~20 min
Brett's daily time commitment

User Journey: B2C

1
Morning Notification
"Today's curiosity drop is live." A single push notification triggers the daily habit loop.
2
Open & Play
3 minutes max. A quirky story setup ("Which of these is actually true?"), a science question, a "What would Brett think?" prediction.
3
Guess, Play, React
Interactive choices, then Brett's reveal with the real answer and his commentary. The testing effect locks it into memory.
4
Score & Streak
See your score, your streak, how you compare to other players. Streak mechanics drive 3x daily return rates.
5
Share
Screenshot your result or forward the wildest story to a friend. Use it at lunch. The Wordle-style shareable grid is what drives the social loop.

User Journey: B2B Add-On

1
Company Deploys
Via Slack/Teams integration or standalone app. Simple admin setup.
2
Daily Curiosity Drop
Employees get a drop during morning or pre-lunch. Mix of Brett's general curation + company-infused industry content.
3
Team Gamification
Team leaderboards, department challenges. Built around the lunch hour so people have something beyond work to talk about.

What Makes It Different

  • Nobody in the creator space is gamifying curation. This format doesn't exist yet.
  • It's active, not passive. You're playing, not just reading.
  • Retention through testing: d = 0.56 effect size. People remember things they get quizzed on (Adesope et al., 2017)
  • "I only got 2/5 today, try to beat me" is a natural social loop, basically the Wordle model
  • Low lift for Brett: ~20 min/day picking stories and writing the setup + reveal. AI handles game mechanics, formatting, and distribution.
  • The B2B layer is what solves monetization. Companies pay a license fee; individuals get free or freemium access.

Why This Matters + Scorecard

This concept hits the mentor's differentiation challenge head-on: the format is the differentiator. No other creator is turning curation into interactive games. And the B2B model answers Brett's sustainability question.

Impact
4/5

This is a new content category, not a remix of something that exists. It gives doom-scrollers something equally addictive but actually useful. The dual B2C/B2B model makes the business viable, and the Berger & Milkman research backs the idea that awe/science content spreads well.

Innovation
5/5

Nothing like this exists. It takes the Duolingo playbook (4.5x DAU growth) and applies it to curated science content, which nobody has tried. The format itself is the moat.

Feasibility
3/5

Needs an interactive front-end (web app or widget), game mechanics design, and B2B integration. That's harder than building a podcast pipeline. We can do a proof-of-concept within I2P, but a full product would need more time.

Business Impact
4/5

The B2B microlearning market is growing from $2.6B to $10.4B by 2032. Revenue comes from two sides: freemium B2C plus licensed B2B at $3–8/user/mo. Kahoot (70% of Fortune 500) and Duolingo ($1B+ revenue) show this model works.

Overall
4.0/5

Next Steps

Riskiest Assumption (RAT)

Will people actually play a daily curiosity game consistently? Is the "dinner table gap" a real pain point, or just a nice theory?

2-Week Experiment

One-week pilot: Brett curates 5 days of stories, we build simple interactive HTML pages, and share them with Brett's "Who's" group.

Go Threshold
>60% completion rate, >20% share rate
No-Go Threshold
<30% completion, no organic sharing

Missing Information

We need user testing on format preference (quiz vs prediction vs story reveal), plus engagement benchmarks from comparable products.

Client Decisions Needed

Is Brett willing to put his content into a game format instead of a traditional podcast? And does the B2B angle interest him?

⚠

Key Risk: This is our most original concept but also the hardest to prototype. There's no precedent to validate the format against, which is why we scored feasibility at 3/5 on purpose.
