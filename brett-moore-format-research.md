# Content Format Research: Primary Format for Brett Moore's Thought Leadership Brand

**Prepared for:** Radical Concepts Sprint 2 Planning
**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Research scope:** Short-form video, long-form video podcast, audio-only podcast, newsletter, and hybrid models for a solo creator targeting professional audiences (35-60, international) in news/geopolitics/science.

---

## 1. Findings

### Audio-Only Podcast: Strong for Depth, Weakening as a Standalone

Audio-only podcasts remain excellent for building deep listener relationships. Professional audiences (executives, managers, business owners) in the 35-54 bracket show 61% monthly podcast listenership, and they engage longer and act on recommendations more than casual listeners (Edison Research, 2025). Branded/niche podcasts achieve 90% completion rates versus just 12% for video content (Casted, 2025). Listeners cite "learning new things" (74%) and "staying up to date" (60%) as top motivations -- directly aligned with Brett's news/geopolitics/science positioning.

However, audio-only is losing ground as a discovery mechanism. YouTube is now the number one podcast platform (33% of U.S. listeners use it as primary), and 53% of new weekly podcast listeners prefer watching over listening (up from 30% in 2022). Audio-only shows are increasingly invisible on the platform where most new listeners discover content.

**Bottom line:** Audio-only builds loyalty with existing listeners but limits discoverability and growth.

### Video Podcast: The Growth Engine, But Production Matters

Video podcasts are the fastest-growing format. YouTube users streamed over 700 million hours of video podcasts on TVs in October 2025, nearly double the prior year (Deloitte TMT Predictions 2026). Video podcast availability has grown 70% year-over-year. Vodcast viewers consume 1.5x more content than audio-only listeners, and podcasts with video receive 47% higher engagement on average (Zebracat, 2025).

Critically, 44% of video podcast watchers never multitask while watching (vs 29% for audio listeners), meaning video commands more focused attention. Deloitte projects global podcast/vodcast ad revenue will reach $5 billion in 2026 (+20% YoY).

The catch: full production video is expensive. But the market has moved toward "talking head" or "camera on desk" formats that require minimal production -- a webcam/phone, decent lighting, and a clean background. This is feasible on a minimal budget.

### Newsletter: High Engagement but Slow Growth

Substack newsletters average 45% open rates and 20% click-through rates -- significantly above general email marketing benchmarks (38.7% average open rate across 4.5 billion newsletters analyzed). Professional/finance-focused newsletters perform even better, consistently hitting 30-50% open rates (Newsletter Valuator, 2026). Newsletters also create an owned audience (email list) that is not subject to algorithm changes.

However, newsletters are slow to grow organically. They lack a native discovery mechanism -- there is no "newsletter algorithm" surfacing your content to new readers. Newsletters work best as a conversion and retention tool rather than a top-of-funnel growth channel.

### Short-Form Video: Discovery, Not Depth

Short-form clips (Reels, Shorts, TikTok) are the most effective format for reach and discovery. 90% of marketers using video report increased brand awareness (SweetFish Media, 2025). The optimal length is 30-60 seconds. But short-form alone does not build thought leadership -- it builds impressions. Podcast audiences spend 20-45 minutes with a host, forming connections that 60-second clips cannot replicate (We Edit Podcasts, 2026).

For Brett's demographic (35-60 professionals), short-form is a funnel entry point, not the destination.

## 2. Recommendation

### Primary format: Video podcast (camera-on, simple setup)

Record each 5-7 minute episode with video on. This is not a production-heavy decision -- it means pointing a phone or webcam at Brett while he records. The audio track serves audio-only platforms (Apple Podcasts, Spotify); the video track goes to YouTube. This single change unlocks the largest podcast discovery platform (YouTube, 33% of listeners) without meaningfully increasing production cost or time.

**Why video-first over audio-first:**
- YouTube is now the number one podcast platform globally. An audio-only show is invisible there.
- 53% of new podcast listeners prefer video. Brett needs new listeners, not just existing ones.
- Video podcasts get 47% higher engagement and 1.5x content consumption vs audio-only.
- Brett's 5-7 minute format is ideal for video -- short enough to hold visual attention, long enough to build credibility.
- At the budget level (a phone on a tripod, natural light, a quiet room), the marginal cost of adding video is near zero.

**Why not newsletter-first or short-form-first:**
- Newsletter lacks a discovery mechanism. Without an existing audience, growth is painfully slow.
- Short-form builds impressions but not the deep trust that thought leadership requires.
- Both work excellently as secondary formats fed by the primary video podcast.

### How the primary format feeds secondary formats (Hub and Spoke)

One recording session produces the following content cascade:

| Step | Output | Platform | Effort |
|------|--------|----------|--------|
| 1. Record | 5-7 min video podcast episode | YouTube, Spotify Video | ~15 min recording |
| 2. Strip audio | Audio-only episode (RSS) | Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Pocket Casts | Automated (hosting platform) |
| 3. AI-clip the 3 topics | 3 standalone 60-90 sec vertical clips with captions | YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn, TikTok | ~20 min with AI tools (Opus Clip, Recast Studio, or CapCut) |
| 4. Pull key quotes | 2-3 text posts per episode | LinkedIn, X/Twitter | ~10 min (AI-assisted from transcript) |
| 5. Weekly digest | Newsletter compiling the week's 3 episodes (9 topics) with Brett's added commentary | Substack or Beehiiv | ~30 min writing/editing per week |

**Total weekly time for 3 episodes:** ~3 hours recording + repurposing for 5+ platforms.

This is the standard "hub and spoke" model (Justin Welsh, Dave Schwertfeger): one long-form asset (the video podcast episode) is the hub; clips, posts, and newsletter issues are the spokes that drive traffic back to the hub. The newsletter specifically converts casual viewers into an owned email list, which is Brett's most durable asset long-term.

### Sprint 2 Phasing

- **Immediate:** Add a phone/webcam to the existing audio recording setup. Publish to YouTube alongside audio platforms.
- **Week 2-3:** Begin clipping each episode into 3 short-form videos using AI tools (Opus Clip free tier or CapCut).
- **Week 4:** Launch a weekly newsletter that curates the week's topics with added context. This builds the email list while the podcast builds the audience.
